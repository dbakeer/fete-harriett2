June 29th 2016

Please join us for a rad evening of women supporting women. The Harriett Buhai Center for Family Law provides women living below the poverty line with the tools they need to navigate the family court system. With the help of volunteers, the Center protects victims of domestic violence and children in poverty by offering free family law assistance and legal education to the poor.

The staff and volunteers at the Center aspire to create a community where poverty is not a barrier to those who seek to resolve critical family law matters and domestic violence in our community no longer exists.

We have brought together a group of strong female hosts from the fashion, art, design, entertainment and legal communities to celebrate and support the work of the Harriet Buhai Center for Family Law. We will dress chic, dance hard and party until midnight, while raising money to support a very worthy cause.

Mission

We protect victims of domestic violence and improve the well-being of children living in poverty. With the help of volunteers, the Center provides free family law assistance and legal education to the poor. We strive to empower people in need and assure them meaningful access to the courts.

Vision

We aspire to create a community where poverty is not a barrier to those who seek to resolve critical family law matters.
